The Rotary Harp

The rotary harp is a musical machine designed to be an automation of the acustic harp. It consists of a two multi-directional spinning pieces, allowing the machine to quickly find and play the correct string. The project was carried out in completion of the WPI Musical Robotics practicum course in a group with two additional students. It is a continuation of the same machine from a previous group, with new design and all new software.

As the sole programmer on this project, I gained great experience in embedded programming. The machine is controlled by a Raspberry Pi Pico W and programmed in Python (MicroPython). It includes features such as Wi-Fi communication over local networks, serial connection for physical communication, precise hardware control including ramping motors and algorithmic selection of closest motors, live playing of notes from a digital audio workstation, and autonomous playing of random notes.

Interactive Hospital Web App

This project, carried out in completion of the WPI Software Engineering course, was done in a team of eleven software engineering students applying the agile development methodology. Over the course of seven weeks, the team created a website to act as an new improved version of the Mass General Brigham website, including a sophisticated map page, user-friendly patient services, and several employee-level services. While the site was deployed via AWS, it is not, and was never meant to be, used as the actual site for the hospital.

In this project I took on the role of scrum master and backend and algorithms engineer. I led the programming of the in-hospital pathfinding, implementing a versatile subsystem with interchangable pathfinding methods. I also contributed to database design and backend programming, culminating in the creation of robust endpoints for the implementation of a user-friendly map editor for hospital layouts, and 3-D maps.

Through this project I honed my skills working with algorithms and pathfinding, learned the basics of full stack website development, got hands-on experience working with agile development, and improved my collaborative abilities in a large, fast-paced, team.

2-D Platforming Game "SHIFT"

SHIFT is a 2-D platforming game created by myself and five other students for the WPI GoatHacks hackathon in 2025, winning Best Game at the event. The game, while unfinished, features simplistic graphics and includes unique gameplay mechanics, such as the ability for the player to travel through time to complete levels, and was built with C# and Unity 6.

In this project, I primarily focused on the development of player and enemy movement, enemy AI, and interactions between the player and their surroundings, such as special collisions and damage tracking. Being my first time working with an engine such as Unity, I gained valuable experience creating an interactive application that saw real-time user input and program feedback.
